Gym Programs

PPL, Upper/Lower, and Powerlifting with smart progression.

Push Pull Legs (PPL)

Rotate Push (chest/shoulders/triceps), Pull (back/biceps), and Legs days 5–6×/week.

  • Push: Bench press, OHP, dips, incline press
  • Pull: Deadlift or row, pull-ups, face pulls
  • Legs: Squat, RDL, lunges, calves

Upper / Lower Split

Train Upper (push + pull) and Lower on alternating days, 4×/week for recovery & gains.

  • Upper: Bench/OHP, rows/pull-ups, isolations
  • Lower: Squat/RDL, lunges, posterior chain

Powerlifting

Focus on squat, bench, deadlift. Apply progressive overload safely.

  • Increase load 2.5–5 kg when all reps are clean
  • Use RPE 7–8 for most work sets
  • Deload every 4–6 weeks
Warm up 10–15 min (joints + light sets).
Prioritize form over weight.
Log your lifts to track progress.

Calisthenics Programs

Master basics, build static skills, add weight when ready.

Reps & Sets Basics

Build capacity with push-ups, pull-ups, dips, squats, core.

  • 3–5 sets × 6–12 reps (strength & hypertrophy)
  • Rest 60–120s between sets
  • Progress: add reps, reduce assistance

Static Skills

Front lever, planche, handstand progressions:

  • Tuck → Advanced tuck → Straddle → Full
  • 10–20s holds × 3–5 sets
  • Balance drills & scapular strength

Weighted Calisthenics

Add plates or a dip belt once bodyweight sets are easy.

  • Dips & pull-ups: 5×5 or 4×6
  • Progress 2.5–5 kg when solid
  • Keep ROM strict & controlled
Progress isn’t luck—it's consistency. Small wins daily.

Nutrition & Diet

Fuel your goal: muscle, fat loss, or detox.

Muscle Gain

  • Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg
  • Carbs: fuel training performance
  • Healthy fats: hormone support

Fat Loss

  • Calorie deficit (−300 to −500 kcal)
  • High protein keeps you full
  • Fiber & steps (8–10k/day)

Detox

  • Hydration, veg/fruit smoothies
  • Light meals, reduce processed foods
  • Sleep & gentle movement

Cardio & Mobility

Run, HIIT, stretch—move better and recover faster.

Running
  • 2–3 × 20–40 min easy pace
  • Build aerobic base
HIIT
  • 10–20 min total, 1–2×/week
  • Work:rest = 20:40 to 30:60
Mobility
  • Daily 5–10 min hips/shoulders
  • After training or on rest days

Tips

Simple rules that compound over time.

Sleep 7–8h

Recovery, hormones, and performance all improve with quality sleep.

Consistency > Intensity

Show up, hit your numbers, and let progression be steady.

Track Progress

Use a simple logbook or app to record lifts, sets, reps, and bodyweight.
